The Experimental Conditions

\[ H_{0}: \mu_{\mathrm{CI}} - \mu_{\mathrm{CI\ rescaled}} = 0 \]

To help players decide, they were randomly shown one of four uncertainty visualizations, each showing the special boulder‚Äôs advantage over the standard one. The key difference is how the uncertainty around the group means is displayed. Today, we‚Äôre focusing on the CI versus Rescaled CI conditions to see if willingness to pay differs. 1: Select & Observe

Pick two conditions & calculate observed difference

To begin the activity, we first pick two groups and compute the observed mean difference plus 95% CIs. This anchors students in the real effect before any shuffling happens.

2: Shuffle Labels to Test the Null

Next, we randomly reassign group labels 5‚ÄØ000 times. The first panel shows the real data; the rest show shuffled labels‚Äîletting students watch the null world form step by step.‚Äù

3: Build the Null Distribution

For each shuffle, we calculate the difference in means. Overlaying these 5‚ÄØ000 differences gives an empirical null distribution ‚Äî that is, our hands‚Äëon sampling distribution under the null hypothesis.

4: Calculate Empirical p-value

What proportion of random shuffles produced effects as large as the real data?

p = 0.048 (4.8% of 5000 permutations)

We simply count the proportion of shuffled differences at least as extreme as what we observed. That fraction is the p‚Äëvalue‚Äîno formulas, just data in action.

Our permutation test shows that 4.8% of 5000 random shuffles produced group differences as extreme as our observed difference (-11.75).

5: Compute the Standard Error

Standard Error (SE) (i.e., SD of null distribution): 5.785

The SD of our null differences is the standard error for the group difference. It shows the typical variation under the null hypothesis.

6: Construct the Test Statistic

Group Difference/SE = Standardized Test Statistic

Divide the observed difference by that SE to get a standardized test statistic. On the null density, we shade the central 95% and mark where our result falls ‚Äî so students see how ‚Äòfar out‚Äô it really is.
